## Pieces
Pawn:
- 8 different pawns
    - White pawns start on row 2, black pawns start on row 7
    - Have no notation during moves
- Movement is simply one move forwards as a regular move
    - Can only capture diagonally right or left one square
    - If the pawn is on the original rank, it can move forward 2 rows but only on the original row
- En passant (see en passant section)
- Value of 1

Knight:
- 2 different knights
    - White starts on b1 and g1, black starts on b8 and g8
    - Notation is k
- Moves in a l shape (two in one direction and then one in another)
    - Can jump over pieces
- Value of 3

Bishop:
- 2 different bishops
    - White starts on c1 and f1, black starts on c8 and f8
    - notation is b
- Moves in diagonal rows
    - One covering white other covering black
- Value of 3

Rook:
- 2 different rooks
    - White starts on a1 and h1, black starts on a8 and h8
    - Notation is r
- Moves in horizontal rows
- Castling (see castling section)
- Value of 5

Queen:
- 1 Queen
    - White starts on d1, lback starts on d8
- Can move diagonally and horizontally to any square (rook and bishop combined)
- Value of 9

King:
- 1 King
    - White starts on e1, black starts on e8
- Can move and capture any square next to it (move one square around it)
- When it can be captured must be moved (check), if there is nowhere to go you lose the game
- Castling (see castling section)
- No inherent value

## Board
- 8x8 (64 board) piece of checked white and black squares
- Numbered from a1-h8
- Moves are notated through piece symbols, squares on board and special move if needed
